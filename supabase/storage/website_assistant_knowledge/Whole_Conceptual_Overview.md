# Whole — Program Overview

**Program:** Whole — A 16-week group coaching and wellness program
**Creator:** Scott Crawford — Associate Certified Coach (ICF, ACC) & Certified Personal Trainer (ISSA), Crawford Coaching
**Location:** Kingston, Ontario (delivered virtually)

---

## What Whole Is

Whole is a 16-week group program that brings together coaching, positive psychology, exercise science, nutrition, and communication skills into a single integrated experience. It's designed for people who want to build sustainable habits around how they move, eat, rest, and relate to themselves and others — but who've found that prescriptive programs, quick fixes, and willpower-based approaches don't stick.

The program doesn't hand participants a plan and send them on their way. It helps them develop the self-awareness, thinking skills, and peer support to figure out what actually works for them — and to keep adjusting as life changes.

---

## Who It's For

Adults — typically between 30 and 60 — who are curious, willing to reflect, and open to learning alongside others. Participants might be navigating career transitions, midlife health shifts, burnout recovery, or simply wanting more from their daily routines. No athletic background or coaching experience is required. The only prerequisite is a willingness to show up and try.

Whole is also designed to be fully accessible. Participants with chronic conditions, injuries, or mobility limitations are welcome and supported. Every recommendation in the program adapts to fit real life — that's a core design principle, not an afterthought.

---

## The Guiding Philosophy

The program is built on a simple idea: **"Do what you can."**

This means consistency over intensity. Progress over perfection. Adapting to where you actually are rather than where you think you should be. It's the antidote to all-or-nothing thinking — the belief that if you can't do it perfectly, there's no point doing it at all.

Whole treats every small choice as meaningful. Health and wellbeing don't turn around overnight — they shift gradually, through the steady accumulation of better decisions. The program teaches participants to trust that process.

## How It Works

### Structure

- **16 weeks**, one 2-hour live session per week
- **Small groups** of 8–12 participants
- **Delivered virtually** via video call
- Weekly materials released progressively (not all at once)
- A shared group channel for mid-week check-ins and encouragement between sessions
- A physical welcome pack including branded materials to start the experience

### The Learning Experience

Each session follows a consistent rhythm: a group check-in, a focused learning module, a peer coaching breakout, a group harvest of insights, and a preview of the coming week's theme.

The learning style is reflective and conversational, not lecture-based. The facilitator presents ideas and frameworks clearly, then invites participants to explore what those ideas mean to them — through guided reflection, small-group discussion, and structured peer coaching. Participants learn by doing, not by being told.

A significant part of the experience happens in small peer coaching groups (triads), where participants practise supporting each other using a simple coaching conversation framework taught within the program. This isn't therapy and it isn't advice-giving — it's learning to ask better questions, listen more deeply, and help someone find their own next step.

### Content Themes

The 16 weeks move through five phases:

1. **Foundation** — Self-awareness, motivation, values, habits, and how change actually works. The program's core thinking tools and coaching skills are introduced here.
2. **Communication** — Emotional literacy and relational skills that deepen self-understanding and transform conversations at home and at work.
3. **The Body** — Movement foundations, strength, and aerobic fitness — grounded in real-life function and longevity, not performance pressure or aesthetics.
4. **Fuel & Recovery** — Sleep, stress management, setback recovery, nutrition fundamentals, and a healthy relationship with food. Education, not prescription.
5. **Integration & Celebration** — Values alignment, identity, systems thinking, and building a personal maintenance plan. All the program's tools are handed over as permanent self-coaching resources.

## What Participants Walk Away With

### Practical Skills
- A personal coaching and goal-setting framework they can use independently for life
- A simple, transferable conversation model that improves any relationship — professional or personal
- Evidence-based knowledge of movement, nutrition, sleep, and recovery tailored to real life
- A self-coaching habit: the ability to check in with themselves, reflect honestly, adjust, and keep going

### Mindset Shifts
- Confidence in their own judgement rather than dependence on external plans or experts
- A healthier relationship with setbacks — understanding that changing a tactic isn't the same as failing a goal
- Greater emotional awareness and the vocabulary to name what they're feeling and what they need
- An identity shift from "someone trying to get healthy" to "someone who lives this way"

### Community
- Lasting peer connections built through 16 weeks of honest, supportive group work
- The experience of being genuinely helpful to others — which reinforces their own growth
- Access to an ongoing support network after the program ends

## What Makes Whole Different

Most wellness programs focus on one domain — fitness, nutrition, or mindset — and hand participants a plan to follow. Whole integrates all of these into a single coherent experience and teaches participants to think for themselves rather than follow instructions.

The program doesn't create dependence on a coach or a system. It builds independence. By the final session, participants own every tool the program uses — not as abstract concepts, but as practised skills they've applied to real challenges over 16 weeks.

The peer coaching component is central, not optional. Participants don't just receive support — they learn to give it. That experience of helping others think more clearly is one of the most powerful drivers of personal growth in the program, and it produces communication skills that transfer directly to work and family life.
